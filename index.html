The Global Education Crisis

Despite global progress in education, millions of children remain excluded from quality learning opportunities due to infrastructure limitations.

Access Gap

260 million children remain out of school globally, with the highest concentrations in Sub-Saharan Africa (32%) and Southern Asia (19%).

Digital Divide

2.9 billion people remain offline globally, with rural connectivity gaps exceeding 63% in least developed countries.

Infrastructure Limitations

759 million people worldwide lack reliable electricity access, making conventional educational technology unusable.

Learning Poverty

70% of children in low- and middle-income countries cannot read and understand a simple text by age 10.

Implementation Roadmap

Our phased approach ensures careful validation and refinement before scaling.

Phase 1: Foundation Building

Months 1-6

Hardware finalization, core software development, initial content creation for 5 languages, pilot site selection, and partnership development.

Phase 2: Pilot Implementation

Months 7-12

Deploy 500 tablets across 5 schools in 2 countries, train 25 teachers, collect real-world usage data, and establish baseline impact measurements.

Phase 3: Validated Scaling

Months 13-24

Expand to 100+ schools across 5 countries, scale manufacturing to 10,000+ units monthly, add support for 15 languages, and establish 3 regional support centers.

Phase 4: Sustainable Growth

Months 25-36

Reach 250,000+ students across 15 countries, support 25+ languages, explore local manufacturing options, and transition to financial sustainability.
